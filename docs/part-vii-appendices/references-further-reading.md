# Appendix D: References and Further Reading

## Overview

This appendix provides a comprehensive collection of references, citations, and recommended reading materials for humanoid robotics development. The field of humanoid robotics draws from multiple disciplines including robotics, artificial intelligence, control theory, computer vision, and mechanical engineering. This reference collection is organized by topic and includes academic papers, books, conference proceedings, online resources, and seminal works that have shaped the field.

The references are categorized by subject area and include both foundational works that established core concepts and recent developments that represent the current state of the art. This appendix serves as a starting point for deeper exploration of specific topics and provides a foundation for continued learning and research in humanoid robotics.

## Standards and Specifications

### Robotics Standards

**ROS 2 Interface Definition Language (IDL):**
- Standard for message and service definitions
- Enables interoperability between different languages
- Part of ROS 2 middleware specification

**URDF (Unified Robot Description Format):**
- Standard for robot model description
- XML-based format for robot kinematics and dynamics
- Widely adopted in the robotics community

**SDF (Simulation Description Format):**
- Standard for simulation environment description
- XML-based format for physics simulation
- Used by Gazebo and other simulation environments

### Industry Standards

**ISO 13482:2014 - Personal Care Robots:**
- Safety requirements for personal care robots
- Includes humanoid and service robots
- Covers risk assessment and safety measures

**ISO 18646-1:2015 - Service Robots:**
- Performance metrics for service robots
- Includes navigation and manipulation capabilities
- Provides standardized testing methods

## Historical and Seminal Works

### Early Humanoid Robotics

**WABOT-1 (Waseda Robot):**
- First full-scale humanoid robot (1973)
- Developed at Waseda University by Prof. Ichiro Kato
- Pioneered many humanoid robotics concepts

**Honda P Series (1986-1997):**
- Series of bipedal walking robots
- Led to the development of ASIMO
- Advanced bipedal locomotion and balance control

**ASIMO (2000):**
- Honda's humanoid robot
- Demonstrated advanced walking and interaction capabilities
- Influenced humanoid robotics development worldwide

### Modern Milestones

**Boston Dynamics Robots:**
- BigDog (2005) - Quadruped robot
- Atlas (2013) - Humanoid robot with dynamic locomotion
- Spot (2016) - Quadruped robot for commercial use

**SoftBank Robotics:**
- NAO (2008) - Humanoid robot for research and education
- Pepper (2014) - Humanoid robot for human interaction
- Widely used in research and commercial applications

**Boston Dynamics Spot:**
- Commercial quadruped robot
- Demonstrates advanced locomotion and autonomy
- Used in various commercial and research applications
